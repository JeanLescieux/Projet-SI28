How to Be Immortal

Digital Immortality

But first, let's define the term "digital immortality" as "the continuation of an active or passive digital presence after death."

One of the first, if not the very first, articles describing "digital immortality" dates back to 2000. In an article titled Digital Immortality, Gordon Bell and Jim Gray speculated that in the future, it will be possible to create a "two-way immortality" where an individual's experiences and learnings would continue to evolve in a digital form after their death. Digital immortality involves converting aspects of a person into information and storing it on a durable medium. The authors anticipate that it will be possible to create avatars or digital versions of individuals who can interact with others, learn, and stay updated with advancements, thus leading to a form of two-way immortality. This could mean that a digital representation of a person could continue to "live and communicate" indefinitely.

One-Way and Two-Way Immortality

One of the key concepts in exploring digital immortality is the concept of one-way and two-way immortality.

"One-way immortality" refers to a form of digital immortality where a person's data, such as their thoughts, memories, and personality, is preserved in a digital form, but there is no ongoing interaction with the outside world. In this model, the digital persona is not designed to learn, adapt, or evolve in response to new interactions after its initial creation. It is a static preservation, similar to a time capsule of a person's digital footprint, existing without the capacity for growth or change. An example of this could be a person's Instagram profile or any other social media profile. It contains information about that person, what they liked, what they created, or their opinions on various topics, but it will not change. It is static, frozen in time.

"Two-way immortality", on the other hand, involves a dynamic form of digital immortality where the digital persona can interact with the living, learn from new experiences, and perhaps even evolve over time. This model suggests a more sophisticated form of digital existence where the persona can engage in interactive relationships with the environment and the people around it, much like a living being. This type of immortality is more complex and raises additional questions about consciousness, identity, and the nature of existence, as the digital persona is not merely a record, but an active participant that can evolve independently from its original human model. This is where human chatbot replicas come into play.
